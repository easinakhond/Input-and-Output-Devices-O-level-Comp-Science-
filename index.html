Input Devices

Device What It Does Where It Is Used Why It Is Used
Barcode ScannerReads barcodes to capture product information.Supermarkets, Warehouses, LibrariesQuickly and accurately retrieves product details for stock and checkout.
Digital CameraCaptures photographs or videos as digital images.Photography, Smartphones, WebcamsHigh-quality visual content.
KeyboardInputs text and commands by pressing keys.Computers, Tablets, LaptopsType documents, enter data, control software.
MicrophoneCaptures analogue sound and converts it into digital signals.Recording studios, Smartphones, ComputersAudio recording, voice communication.
Optical MouseMoves the cursor on the screen based on hand movement.Computers, LaptopsPrecise control and navigation on a computer screen.
QR Code ScannerReads QR codes to access stored information.Retail shops, Mobile appsQuickly retrieves data from QR codes (websites, product info).
Touch Screen (Resistive)Detects touch by pressing a flexible screen layer.ATMs, Older smartphones and tabletsDirect interaction with the device.
Touch Screen (Infra-red)Detects touch by interrupting infrared light beams across the screen.Kiosks, Large touch displaysAccurate touch detection, works in various lighting conditions.
Touch Screen (Capacitive)Detects touch through changes in electrical charge on the screen surface.Modern smartphones, Tablets, Touch monitorsSmooth, responsive touch experience with multi-touch capabilities.
Two-Dimensional (2D) ScannerCaptures flat, 2D images of documents or objects.Offices, Libraries, SchoolsDigitizes documents and photos for storage, editing, or sharing.
Three-Dimensional (3D) ScannerCaptures detailed 3D shapes and dimensions of objects.Manufacturing, Design, Medical imagingCreates digital models of objects for design, analysis, or reproduction.

Output Devices

Name of Device What Does It Do? Where Is It Used? Why Is It Used?
ActuatorConverts energy into motion; performs mechanical tasks.Making robots, Control valves, Smart home automationEssential in any system that requires movement.
DLP ProjectorProjects images or videos by shining light through tiny mirrors.Cinemas, Classrooms, Large presentationsHigh-quality large-screen visuals.
Inkjet PrinterPrints text and images onto paper by spraying tiny droplets of ink.Home, Schools, OfficesLow cost compared to other types of printers.
Laser PrinterHeats toner (a fine powder) onto the paper to create prints.Offices, Schools, HomeHigh-quality prints, fast print speed.
LED ScreenDisplays images and videos using tiny light bulbs called LEDs.Televisions, Computers, SmartphonesHigh-quality visuals, energy-efficient, thin.
LCD ProjectorProjects images or video through a transparent panel illuminated by light.Classrooms, Conference halls, CinemasBright, efficient balance of color and clarity in images.
LCD ScreenDisplays images and video using liquid crystals with a backlight.Televisions, Computers, SmartphonesEnergy efficient, lightweight.
SpeakerConverts electrical signals into analogue sound waves.Radios, Entertainment systems, SmartphonesMultiple shapes and sizes for different uses.
3D PrinterAdds thin layers of plastic, metal, or other material to form a 3D object.Manufacturing, Education, HomeCreates prototypes, custom parts.
